Again, Germany shows the lowest results with values for cost of debt ranging between 1.8 and 3.2 with a falling tendency in 2015. According to German experts, another reason for the very low values is the abovementioned competition between German banks many banks have come to consider wind energy projects as secure investments and underbid each other. As a result, German project developers face much lower costs of debt than developers in countries with less competition. The cost of debt is currently featuring a falling tendency caused by postcrisis measures, resulting in declining EIB loans and EURIBOR. What was surprising  and quite alarming  was that, in some countries, the values for the cost of debt were found to be substantially higher than in the model results. Among these countries are Romania, Bulgaria, Italy and Spain.
