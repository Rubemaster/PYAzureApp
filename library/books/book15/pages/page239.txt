The Strait of Gibraltar is located in the southwestern extreme of Spain. This area separates southernmost Spain from northernmost Morocco and represents the shortest distance between continental Europe and North Africa (approximately 14 km), acting as an obligatory crossing point for most soaring bird migrants from Western Europe. Almost half a million soaring birds and 3050 million small birds cross the Strait of Gibraltar every year to and from their winter quarters in Africa (Migres Foundation, unpublished data).
